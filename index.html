Y2K Aesthetic

Y2K (also known as Kaybug or Cybercore) is an aesthetic that was prevalent in popular culture from roughly 1997 to 2004, succeeding the Memphis Design and Grunge eras and overlapping with the McBling, UrBling, Surf Crush, and 2K1 aesthetics.

Neo Brutalism

NeuBrutalism, NeoBrutalism is attractive in its concept of rejecting the rules of traditional UI design in color theory and elemental alignment. Features include unusual background colors, modern typography, animation, strong contrast, dark contours, hard shadows, and random layouts.
